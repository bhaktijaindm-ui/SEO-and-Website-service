Breaking Down the 2025 Speed Framework: INP is Here

Google recently replaced FID with Interaction to Next Paint (INP). Most agencies are still catching up, but our INP fix strategy has been part of our core dashboard for over a year. INP measures the responsiveness of your entire user journey, not just the first click. If your mobile menu takes 300ms to open, you're failing. User experience SEO is now a direct metric of your code's quality, not just your branding.

Our LCP improvement playbook focuses on pre-loading critical assets and implementing edge-caching across global CDNs. By the time a user thinks about clicking, your content is already rendered. Vitals & Speed: FAQ

What are the three Core Web Vitals?

Google focuses on LCP (Loading speed), INP (Responsiveness), and CLS (Visual stability). Together, these form the core of his Page Experience ranking signals.

How does LCP affect my SEO?

LCP is a direct ranking signal. Pages that load under 2.5 seconds are prioritized. Slow pages increase bounce rates and lower your "Content Quality" score in Google's eyes.

What is INP and why is it important now?

Interaction to Next Paint (INP) replaced FID. It measures how long it takes for a page to respond to user interactions. High INP causes frustration and leads to lower mobile rankings.

Can a slow site still rank #1?

Only if its content is vastly superior to competitors. However, in competitive niches, a fast site will win every single time. Google treats Core Web Vitals as a tie-breaker that often decides the top 3 spots.

How do I fix Layout Shift (CLS)?

You fix it by declaring size attributes for all images/videos and ensuring ads or embeds don't dynamically push content after the page has finished loading.

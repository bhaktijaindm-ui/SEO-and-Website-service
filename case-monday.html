The Mission: Go Big or Go Home

In 2020, Monday.com was preparing for their IPO. They needed a massive, sustainable organic moat to compete with giants like Asana and Trello. Instead of slow experimentation, they chose aggressive scale—building a "Content Factory" designed to produce 1,000 top-tier SEO articles in a single year.

"The head of acquisitions' mindset was to just go big or go home. And that's really what you saw." — Zoe Averbuch, Senior SEO Manager at Monday.com

The Technical Architecture

01
The "Planning Predictor" Instead of generic keyword research, they mapped keywords based on buyer intent. They prioritized "Commercial Intent" terms like "construction project management software" that had immediate trial conversion potential.

Niche-Expert Writing Army They vetted 1,500+ writers to find 15 specialists. Each specialist was assigned a specific niche (HR, Dev, Sales) to ensure the content wasn't just SEO-friendly, but genuinely authoritative.
